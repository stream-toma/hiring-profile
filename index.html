Team Hiring Profile

Who We Want

Smart, adaptable people with intellectual horsepower who can think through ambiguity.

We Hire For

  • Logical reasoning – can structure thinking, not paralyzed by incomplete information
  • Ownership – takes initiative, cares beyond their job description
  • Growth mindset – has visibly improved, honest about weaknesses
  • Flexibility – adapts to different people and situations

We Avoid

  • Extreme personalities who can't flex
  • People who need heavy structure to function
  • "That's not my job" mentality

The Kind of People We Hire

Smart Without Being Rigid

Our people are genuinely intelligent, but intelligence here doesn't mean knowing everything or being the smartest person in the room. It means being able to think clearly when things are unclear. They structure problems, find patterns, and reason through ambiguity without freezing. They're confident enough to make a call with incomplete information, and humble enough to adjust when they learn more.

Balanced, Not Extreme

Look at our personality data and you'll notice something: almost everyone has 2-3 traits sitting in the 55-65% range. This isn't coincidence. We hire people who can flex. They're analytical enough to engage with hard problems but not so rigid they can't collaborate with different styles. They have strong opinions, but they hold them loosely. Extremists – people who are 90%+ on multiple dimensions – struggle here because they can't adapt.

Ownership Is Instinctive

The people who thrive here don't wait to be told what to do. They see something broken and fix it. They see something that could be better and improve it. This isn't about heroics or working overtime – it's about caring. They take pride in their work and feel genuine responsibility for outcomes, not just tasks. "That's not my job" is a phrase you'll never hear.

Grounded Visionaries

We're all intuitive thinkers – we see patterns, possibilities, and the big picture. But we're not dreamers. Our intuition is grounded (most of us are 54-70% on that scale). We think strategically without losing touch with what's actually achievable. We're not building castles in the sky; we're building things that ship.

Logic First, But Not Logic Only

The team runs on analytical thinking. Even our "Feeling" types score moderate – they can engage with logical arguments and be persuaded by evidence. But we're not robots. We have people who bring warmth, who build relationships, who notice when someone's struggling. The difference is that emotion complements the logic rather than overriding it.

Drive Without Drama

Some of our best people have that slightly restless quality – the Turbulent types who are never quite satisfied, always pushing to improve. But it's channeled into the work, not into anxiety or conflict. Others are rock-solid Assertive types who stay calm when everything's on fire. We need both. What we don't need is drama, ego, or people who make everything about themselves.

They Get Better

This team develops people. Some of our strongest contributors weren't fully formed when they joined – they grew into reliability, into leadership, into expertise. We look for trajectory, not just current state. Someone who was bad at something a year ago and is good at it now is more interesting than someone who's always been "fine" at everything.
